In Lord of the Rings: Fellowship of the Ring we are introduced to a young hobbit named Frodo Baggins, nephew of famed hobbit Bilbo Baggins. Frodo inherits a powerful and dangerous ring from Bilbo, one that must be destroyed to prevent the dark lord Sauron from conquering Middle Earth. Frodo, alongside three hobbits, two men, an elf, and a wizard, form the Fellowship of the Ring to bring the Ring to the fiery pits of Mordor (the only place it can be destroyed). Along the way they face many different perils such as treacherous landscapes, dark creatures, and the corrupting influence of the Ring itself. In the end, the Fellowship is broken as Boromir succumbs to the Ring's power which forces Frodo and Sam to depart on the mission to Mordor alone. The Fellowship of the Ring ends with the Fellowship divided, yet determined to finish the quest they set out on.
